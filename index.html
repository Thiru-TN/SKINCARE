What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Conditions and Diseases

Dry Skin

Dry skin lacks moisture and can feel tight, itchy, and rough. It may be caused by environmental factors, genetics, or certain medical conditions.

Acne

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It often causes pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads.

Acne Scars

Acne scars are marks left on the skin after the healing of acne lesions. They can vary in appearance and may be temporary or permanent.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is a condition that causes hair loss in small, round patches on the scalp or other areas of the body. It is thought to be an autoimmune disorder.

Acanthosis Nigricans

Acanthosis nigricans is a skin condition characterized by dark, thickened patches of skin, often occurring in the folds of the skin. It can be a sign of insulin resistance or certain medical conditions.

Melasma

Melasma is a common skin condition that causes brown or gray patches to develop on the skin, usually on the face. It is often associated with hormonal changes and sun exposure.

Vitiligo

Vitiligo is a skin disorder characterized by the loss of skin color in patches. It occurs when melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing skin pigment, are destroyed.

Warts

Warts are small, benign growths on the skin caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). They can appear anywhere on the body and are contagious.
